,"articleBody": "&lt;p&gt;When applying for certain positions in the US, as well as jobs internationally, you may be required to submit a curriculum vitae rather than a resume. A curriculum vitae, or CV, includes more information than your typical resume, including details of your education and academic achievements, research, publications, awards, affiliations, and more.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Here you can review curriculum vitae samples, learn about the &lt;a href=&quot;https://www.thebalancecareers.com/cv-vs-resume-2058495&quot; data-component=&quot;link&quot; data-source=&quot;inlineLink&quot; data-type=&quot;internalLink&quot; data-ordinal=&quot;1&quot;&gt;difference between a CV and a resume&lt;/a&gt;, and glean tips and advice on how to write a CV.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h3&gt;What to Include in a Curriculum Vitae&lt;/h3&gt;
&lt;p&gt;A curriculum vitae, commonly referred to as a CV, is a longer (two or more pages), more detailed synopsis than a resume. Your CV should be clear, concise, complete, and up-to-date with current employment and educational information.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The following are examples of information that can be included in your curriculum vitae. The elements that you include will depend on what you are applying for, so be sure to incorporate the most relevant information to support your candidacy in your CV.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;ul&gt;&lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Personal details and contact information.&lt;/strong&gt; Most CVs start with contact information and personal data but take care to avoid superfluous details, such as religious affiliation, children&amp;#39;s names, and so on.&lt;/li&gt;&lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Education and qualifications&lt;/strong&gt;. Be sure to include the names of institutions and dates attended in reverse&lt;strong&gt; &lt;/strong&gt;order: Ph.D., Masters, Undergraduate.&lt;/li&gt;&lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Work experience/employment history&lt;/strong&gt;. The most widely accepted style of employment record is the &lt;a href=&quot;https://www.thebalancecareers.com/what-is-a-chronological-resume-2061944&quot; data-component=&quot;link&quot; data-source=&quot;inlineLink&quot; data-type=&quot;internalLink&quot; data-ordinal=&quot;1&quot;&gt;chronological curriculum vitae&lt;/a&gt;. Your &lt;a href=&quot;https://www.thebalancecareers.com/how-to-find-your-employment-history-2060696&quot; data-component=&quot;link&quot; data-source=&quot;inlineLink&quot; data-type=&quot;internalLink&quot; data-ordinal=&quot;2&quot;&gt;career history&lt;/a&gt; is presented in reverse date order starting with the most recent appointment. More emphasis/information should be placed on your most recent jobs.&lt;/li&gt;&lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Skills&lt;/strong&gt;. Include computer skills, foreign language skills, and any other recent training that is relevant to the role applied for.&lt;/li&gt;&lt;li&gt;Training / Graduate Fieldwork / Study Abroad&lt;/li&gt;&lt;li&gt;Dissertations / Theses&lt;/li&gt;&lt;li&gt;Research experience&lt;/li&gt;&lt;li&gt;Teaching experience&lt;/li&gt;&lt;li&gt;Publications&lt;/li&gt;&lt;li&gt;Presentations, lectures, and exhibitions&lt;/li&gt;&lt;li&gt;Grants, scholarships, fellowships, and assistantships&lt;/li&gt;&lt;li&gt;Awards and honors&lt;/li&gt;&lt;li&gt;Technical, computer, and language skills&lt;/li&gt;&lt;li&gt;Professional licenses, certifications, and memberships&lt;/li&gt;&lt;/ul&gt;
&lt;h3&gt;What Not to Include&lt;/h3&gt;
&lt;p&gt;There is no need to include your photo, your &lt;a href=&quot;https://www.thebalancecareers.com/providing-salary-history-to-employers-2063438&quot; data-component=&quot;link&quot; data-source=&quot;inlineLink&quot; data-type=&quot;internalLink&quot; data-ordinal=&quot;1&quot;&gt;salary history&lt;/a&gt;, the reason you left your previous position, or references in a CV submitted for jobs in the United States. &lt;a href=&quot;https://www.thebalancecareers.com/sample-reference-list-for-employment-2062940&quot; data-component=&quot;link&quot; data-source=&quot;inlineLink&quot; data-type=&quot;internalLink&quot; data-ordinal=&quot;2&quot;&gt;References should be listed separately&lt;/a&gt; and given to employers upon request.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Note, however, that the requirements for international CVs differ, and depend upon the country to which you are applying. In other countries, private information like your date of birth, nationality, marital status, how many children you have, and a photograph may be required.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h3&gt;How Long Should a CV Be?&lt;/h3&gt;
&lt;p&gt;A good, entry-level curriculum vitae should ideally cover two to three pages (CVs for mid-level professionals, especially in academia and medical research roles, may run longer). Aim to ensure the content is clear, structured, concise, and relevant. &lt;a href=&quot;https://www.thebalancecareers.com/how-to-include-bullet-points-in-a-resume-2063124&quot; data-component=&quot;link&quot; data-source=&quot;inlineLink&quot; data-type=&quot;internalLink&quot; data-ordinal=&quot;1&quot;&gt;Using bullet points&lt;/a&gt; rather than full sentences can help minimize word usage.&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;h3&gt;Curriculum Vitae Writing Tips&lt;/h3&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Have Several Versions of Your CV&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;br/&gt;Don&amp;#39;t just write one CV and use it for every position you apply for.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;div class=&quot;theme-experttipimportant&quot;&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Have targeted and focused versions of your curriculum vitae and use them accordingly.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;/div&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Keep It Short &lt;/strong&gt;&lt;br/&gt;If possible, try to keep your CV short and concise. Include summaries of your employment and education, rather than lots of details. Use formal (no slang or abbreviations) language, writing simply and clearly.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Tell the Truth &lt;/strong&gt;&lt;br/&gt;It can be tempting to over-polish a CV and make our educational qualifications or work history sound a little better than they are. If you&amp;#39;re tempted to stretch the truth about your &lt;a href=&quot;https://www.thebalancecareers.com/work-history-definition-with-examples-2060468&quot; data-component=&quot;link&quot; data-source=&quot;inlineLink&quot; data-type=&quot;internalLink&quot; data-ordinal=&quot;1&quot;&gt;work history&lt;/a&gt; - don&amp;#39;t. It will come back to haunt you.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Most employers conduct &lt;a href=&quot;https://www.thebalancecareers.com/will-employers-check-your-references-2060797&quot; data-component=&quot;link&quot; data-source=&quot;inlineLink&quot; data-type=&quot;internalLink&quot; data-ordinal=&quot;1&quot;&gt;reference and background checks&lt;/a&gt;, and if your curriculum vitae doesn&amp;#39;t match your actual work history or education, you will most likely get caught at some point either you will be cut as a candidate or you will get fired if you have already been hired.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Check the Format&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;br/&gt;Look at the format of your curriculum vitae. Is there plenty of white space? Is it cluttered? Is your formatting consistent (bold, italic, spacing, etc.) and is the overall picture that your CV provides a professional and polished one?&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Proof Your Curriculum Vitae&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;br/&gt;Double-check your curriculum vitae for typos and grammatical errors. Then, ask someone else to review it for you - it&amp;#39;s often hard to catch our mistakes.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;When to Use a Curriculum Vitae Instead of a Resume&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;br/&gt;In the United States, a curriculum vitae is used when applying for academic, education, scientific, or research positions. A curriculum vitae can also be used to apply for fellowships or grants. In Europe, the Middle East, Africa, or Asia, employers may expect to receive a curriculum vitae rather than a resume.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Choose an Appropriate Curriculum Vitae Format&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;br/&gt;Make sure you &lt;a href=&quot;https://www.thebalancecareers.com/curriculum-vitae-format-2060351&quot; data-component=&quot;link&quot; data-source=&quot;inlineLink&quot; data-type=&quot;internalLink&quot; data-ordinal=&quot;1&quot;&gt;choose a curriculum vitae format&lt;/a&gt; that is appropriate for the position you are applying for. If you are applying for a fellowship, for example, you won&amp;#39;t need to include the personal information that may be included in an international CV.&lt;/p&gt;

<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html">
<p>When applying for certain positions in the US, as well as jobs internationally, you may be required to submit a curriculum vitae rather than a resume. A curriculum vitae, or CV, includes more information than your typical resume, including details of your education and academic achievements, research, publications, awards, affiliations, and more.</p>
</div>
<!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html -->
<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-1" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block"> </div>
<!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block -->
<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-2" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html">
<p>Here you can review curriculum vitae samples, learn about the <a href="https://www.thebalancecareers.com/cv-vs-resume-2058495" data-component="link" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" data-ordinal="1">difference between a CV and a resume</a>, and glean tips and advice on how to write a CV.</p>
</div>
<!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html -->
<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-3" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block"> </div>
<!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block -->
<a class="heading-toc" id="what-to-include-in-a-curriculum-vitae"></a> <h3 id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-4" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-heading"> <span class="mntl-sc-block-heading__text"> What to Include in a Curriculum Vitae </span> </h3> <!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-heading -->
<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-5" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html">
<p>A curriculum vitae, commonly referred to as a CV, is a longer (two or more pages), more detailed synopsis than a resume. Your CV should be clear, concise, complete, and up-to-date with current employment and educational information.</p>
</div>
<!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html -->
<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-6" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block"> </div>
<!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block -->
<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-7" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html">
<p>The following are examples of information that can be included in your curriculum vitae. The elements that you include will depend on what you are applying for, so be sure to incorporate the most relevant information to support your candidacy in your CV.</p>
</div>
<!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html -->
<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-8" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block"> </div>
<!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block -->
<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-9" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html">
<ul><li><strong>Personal details and contact information.</strong> Most CVs start with contact information and personal data but take care to avoid superfluous details, such as religious affiliation, children&#39;s names, and so on.</li><li><strong>Education and qualifications</strong>. Be sure to include the names of institutions and dates attended in reverse<strong> </strong>order: Ph.D., Masters, Undergraduate.</li><li><strong>Work experience/employment history</strong>. The most widely accepted style of employment record is the <a href="https://www.thebalancecareers.com/what-is-a-chronological-resume-2061944" data-component="link" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" data-ordinal="1">chronological curriculum vitae</a>. Your <a href="https://www.thebalancecareers.com/how-to-find-your-employment-history-2060696" data-component="link" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" data-ordinal="2">career history</a> is presented in reverse date order starting with the most recent appointment. More emphasis/information should be placed on your most recent jobs.</li></ul>
</div>
<!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html -->
<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-10" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block"> </div>
<!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block -->
<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-11" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html mntl-sc-block-html-sliced">
<ul class="html-slice"><li><strong>Skills</strong>. Include computer skills, foreign language skills, and any other recent training that is relevant to the role applied for.</li><li>Training / Graduate Fieldwork / Study Abroad</li><li>Dissertations / Theses</li><li>Research experience</li><li>Teaching experience</li><li>Publications</li><li>Presentations, lectures, and exhibitions</li><li>Grants, scholarships, fellowships, and assistantships</li><li>Awards and honors</li><li>Technical, computer, and language skills</li><li>Professional licenses, certifications, and memberships</li></ul>
</div>
<!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html mntl-sc-block-html-sliced -->
<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-12" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block"> </div>
<!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block -->
<a class="heading-toc" id="what-not-to-include"></a> <h3 id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-13" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-heading"> <span class="mntl-sc-block-heading__text"> What Not to Include </span> </h3> <!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-heading -->
<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-14" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html">
<p>There is no need to include your photo, your <a href="https://www.thebalancecareers.com/providing-salary-history-to-employers-2063438" data-component="link" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" data-ordinal="1">salary history</a>, the reason you left your previous position, or references in a CV submitted for jobs in the United States. <a href="https://www.thebalancecareers.com/sample-reference-list-for-employment-2062940" data-component="link" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" data-ordinal="2">References should be listed separately</a> and given to employers upon request.</p>
</div>
<!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html -->
<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-15" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block"> </div>
<!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block -->
<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-16" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html">
<p>Note, however, that the requirements for international CVs differ, and depend upon the country to which you are applying. In other countries, private information like your date of birth, nationality, marital status, how many children you have, and a photograph may be required.</p>
</div>
<!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html -->
<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-17" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block"> </div>
<!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block -->
<a class="heading-toc" id="how-long-should-a-cv-be"></a> <h3 id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-18" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-heading"> <span class="mntl-sc-block-heading__text"> How Long Should a CV Be? </span> </h3> <!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-heading -->
<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-19" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html">
<p>A good, entry-level curriculum vitae should ideally cover two to three pages (CVs for mid-level professionals, especially in academia and medical research roles, may run longer). Aim to ensure the content is clear, structured, concise, and relevant. <a href="https://www.thebalancecareers.com/how-to-include-bullet-points-in-a-resume-2063124" data-component="link" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" data-ordinal="1">Using bullet points</a> rather than full sentences can help minimize word usage.</p>
</div>

<a class="heading-toc" id="curriculum-vitae-writing-tips"></a> <h3 id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-33" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-heading"> <span class="mntl-sc-block-heading__text"> Curriculum Vitae Writing Tips </span> </h3> <!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-heading -->
<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-34" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html">
<p><strong>Have Several Versions of Your CV</strong><br/>Don&#39;t just write one CV and use it for every position you apply for.</p>
</div>
<!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html -->
<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-35" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block"> </div>
<!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block -->
<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-36" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-callout mntl-block theme-experttipimportant">
<div id="mntl-sc-block-callout-body_1-0-1" class="comp mntl-sc-block-callout-body mntl-text-block">
<p>Have targeted and focused versions of your curriculum vitae and use them accordingly.</p> </div>
<!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block-callout-body mntl-text-block -->
</div>
<!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-callout mntl-block theme-experttipimportant -->
<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-37" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html">
<p><strong>Keep It Short </strong><br/>If possible, try to keep your CV short and concise. Include summaries of your employment and education, rather than lots of details. Use formal (no slang or abbreviations) language, writing simply and clearly.</p>
</div>
<!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html -->
<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-38" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block"> </div>
<!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block -->
<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-39" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html">
<p><strong>Tell the Truth </strong><br/>It can be tempting to over-polish a CV and make our educational qualifications or work history sound a little better than they are. If you&#39;re tempted to stretch the truth about your <a href="https://www.thebalancecareers.com/work-history-definition-with-examples-2060468" data-component="link" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" data-ordinal="1">work history</a> - don&#39;t. It will come back to haunt you.</p>
</div>
<!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html -->
<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-40" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block"> </div>
<!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block -->
<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-41" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html">
<p>Most employers conduct <a href="https://www.thebalancecareers.com/will-employers-check-your-references-2060797" data-component="link" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" data-ordinal="1">reference and background checks</a>, and if your curriculum vitae doesn&#39;t match your actual work history or education, you will most likely get caught at some point either you will be cut as a candidate or you will get fired if you have already been hired.</p>
</div>
<!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html -->
<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-42" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block"> </div>
<!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block -->
<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-43" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html">
<p><strong>Check the Format</strong><br/>Look at the format of your curriculum vitae. Is there plenty of white space? Is it cluttered? Is your formatting consistent (bold, italic, spacing, etc.) and is the overall picture that your CV provides a professional and polished one?</p>
</div>
<!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html -->
<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-44" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block"> </div>
<!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block -->
<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-45" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html">
<p><strong>Proof Your Curriculum Vitae</strong><br/>Double-check your curriculum vitae for typos and grammatical errors. Then, ask someone else to review it for you - it&#39;s often hard to catch our mistakes.</p>
</div>
<!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html -->
<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-46" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block"> </div>
<!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block -->
<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-47" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html">
<p><strong>When to Use a Curriculum Vitae Instead of a Resume</strong><br/>In the United States, a curriculum vitae is used when applying for academic, education, scientific, or research positions. A curriculum vitae can also be used to apply for fellowships or grants. In Europe, the Middle East, Africa, or Asia, employers may expect to receive a curriculum vitae rather than a resume.</p>
</div>
<!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html -->
<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-48" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block"> </div>
<!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block -->
<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-49" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html">
<p><strong>Choose an Appropriate Curriculum Vitae Format</strong><br/>Make sure you <a href="https://www.thebalancecareers.com/curriculum-vitae-format-2060351" data-component="link" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" data-ordinal="1">choose a curriculum vitae format</a> that is appropriate for the position you are applying for. If you are applying for a fellowship, for example, you won&#39;t need to include the personal information that may be included in an international CV.</p>
</div>
<!-- end: comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html -->
